Anthropic Raises $50B, Targets $900B Valuation Ahead of IPO

Anthropic has launched a $50 billion pre-IPO funding round, targeting a $900 billion valuation鈥攑otentially making it the most valuable AI company after OpenAI.

Explosive Revenue Growth

Claude's business is growing at an unprecedented pace:

  • February 2026: $14 billion annualized revenue
  • April 2026: $30 billion annualized revenue
  • May 2026: $44 billion annualized revenue (current)

That's a 3x increase in just three months, far outpacing OpenAI's growth rate during the same period.

Why Investors Are Betting Big

Several factors are driving the massive valuation:

  • Enterprise Trust: Claude's safety-focused approach resonates with large enterprises
  • Coding Excellence: Claude Opus 4.7 is widely regarded as the best coding model
  • API Revenue: Strong developer adoption through API and Claude Code
  • Google Partnership: Multi-gigawatt TPU deal ensures compute supply

IPO Timeline

Sources indicate Anthropic plans to go public in late 2026, targeting a Nasdaq listing. The IPO is expected to raise over $60 billion, potentially the largest tech IPO in history. Some early investors are reportedly passing on this round to cash out during the IPO.

Competition Intensifies

The AI model market is becoming a two-horse race. OpenAI recently raised $110 billion in February 2026 at a $3+ trillion valuation. Together, OpenAI and Anthropic account for the vast majority of enterprise AI spending.

Google (Gemini), Meta (Llama), and Mistral remain competitive but haven't captured similar enterprise revenue. The question for investors: is there room for more than two mega-model companies?

What's Next

Anthropic recently released Claude Opus 4.7 and Claude Mythos (a security-focused model). The company has also secured the largest compute deal in its history with Google and Broadcom, ensuring infrastructure for years to come.
